    The key of making a rocket was to design its fins. Using rocket design app, my partner and I first modeled different shapes of fins and the number of fins. Through test, we found that four elliptical fins would make the rocket fly the highest. Then, we used threads, elastic bands, and plastic bags to make a simple parachute.
    It was a sunny breezy day when we were finally going to test our rocket! Although the parachute failed to open in the air, our rocket's height and stability were good. After calculating, our maximum height was 287 feet. After reflection, we thought that the parachute did not successfully open because the threads tangled together in the rocket tube and it took too long to unravel the threads in the air.

    Building a bridge was not a easy thing, but studying one could be even more intricate! Using all the jonts and members printed by the laser-cutting machine, my partners and I followed the pattern on the blueprint to make a truss bridge. As we tested the bridge, it was able to hold 10kg loads at least. However, what was the maximum weight the truss bridge can hold? What was the maximum weight each member can hold? To study these questions, we decided to keep adding loads on the bridge until it failed.
    When the loads added up to 20kg, finally, our bridge collapsed! After four experiments, we found it was not accidental that the end posts of the bridges always buckled first. This demonstrated that the end posts were the weakest member in the truss bridge structure. We were also able to calculate each member's maximum loads using physics.
